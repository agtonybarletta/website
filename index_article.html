Blogs and Me, a complicated Story

When I learned about blog for the first time I just started using internet. After having my PC for years and never being able to see what on the other side of the program with the "e" icon (Internet Explorer on Windows XP), I got an internet connection. It was a mobile router with a pretty slow mobile phone connection, but it was enough.

As all new internet used do I stated to browser the internet and one website came up often. It was a tech website and had a lot of guide on how to do stuff on PC. It was create by a single guy, "Salvatore Aranzulla". It got my attention when I discovered that we made some money with that website.

I wanted to open a website too, but I didn't know anything about the Web. I stating googling and I found a website that allowed anyone to open a blog (blogger.com). It wasn't what I wanted but creating a blog could be a start and so I decided to open it.

It was the most empty blog ever. I wrote a single article with 100 words. After a while I eve forgot that I had a blog.

Years later I discovered tumblr, a website of "micro-blogging". It was a mix of every social network of the time (facebook, myspace etc) and a blogging website. The Italian community of tumbler was pretty nice and got cought in it. I registered, started follow some people and shared some thoughs. It Didn't had success tough because I didn't write much there too.

Luke smith and how Internet supposed to be

A couple of moths ago I start watching a Youtuber, Luke Smith. He does pretty good linux related videos. He also talk about other stuffs in his "rants in the wood" series, where he rants about the current society while filming himself in the middle of the woods.

In one of his (rants in the woods)1 he talks about how everyone should have a website.

He says that Internet it supposed to be a connection of computers, real people computers, that share information they find interesting and communicate between. In the plan of those how designed it the was no big company.

In his video Luke suggests to everyone to go and publish their own website, even to those how are not geeks, because after all it is not that difficult to do it.

I thought that if anyone can do that, why someone like me, should not ?

So that's why I write my little plain html website, and that's why I wrote this blog.
